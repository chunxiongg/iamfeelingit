After delivery, mothers are recommended to breastfeed their children. The World Health Organization recommends exclusive breastfeeding up to at least 6 months. However, during the initial stage, most mothers are often faced with common issues such as engorgement, blocked milk ducts, oversupply of milk, mastitis and more.

In this tab, we will be addressing these concerns while providing tips and solutions.

Disclaimer: Different mothers have different breastfeeding experience, while there are plenty of advice, it is more important for mothers to explore the different solutions to see what suits them best and what not. Breastfeeding should be a comfortable journey and breast milk is the most recommended milk for the baby. If in doubt, mothers should always go to a lactation consultant.
